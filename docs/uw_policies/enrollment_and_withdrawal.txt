UNIVERSITY OF WASHINGTON
ENROLLMENT, DROP, AND WITHDRAWAL POLICY
Office of the Registrar — Updated August 2023

1. REGISTRATION PERIODS

1.1 Advance Registration
Continuing students register during Advance Registration, which opens approximately two weeks before the start of each quarter. Registration priority is assigned based on the number of credits completed: students with more credits register earlier.

1.2 Open Registration
Open Registration begins on the first day of the quarter. All eligible students may add courses that still have open seats.

1.3 Late Registration
Students may register after the second week of the quarter only with written permission from the instructor and the department offering the course. A $25 late registration fee applies.

2. CREDIT LOAD

2.1 Full-Time Status
A student is considered full-time if enrolled in 12 or more credits per quarter for undergraduates, or 10 or more credits for graduate students.

2.2 Maximum Credits
Undergraduate students may enroll in a maximum of 19 credits per quarter without special permission. To enroll in 20 or more credits, students must have a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher and obtain approval from their academic adviser.

2.3 Minimum Credits for Financial Aid
Students receiving federal financial aid must maintain enrollment in at least 6 credits per quarter to remain eligible for aid disbursement.

3. DROPPING COURSES

3.1 Drop Without Record (Weeks 1–2)
Courses dropped during the first two weeks of the quarter do not appear on the student's academic transcript. No tuition refund is available after the end of the first week.

3.2 Drop With W Grade (Weeks 3–7)
Courses dropped between the third and seventh week of the quarter receive a grade of W (Withdrawn) on the transcript. A W grade does not affect GPA but counts toward the total credits attempted.

3.3 Late Drop (After Week 7)
After the seventh week, dropping a course requires written approval from the instructor, the department chair, and the student's academic dean. Late drops are granted only for serious and compelling reasons (medical emergency, family crisis, military deployment). A W grade is assigned.

3.4 Medical Withdrawal
A student experiencing a documented medical condition may request a Medical Withdrawal from all courses at any point in the quarter. Supporting documentation from a licensed healthcare provider is required. Tuition may be refunded on a prorated basis.

4. TUITION REFUND SCHEDULE

Week 1: 100% refund
Week 2: 80% refund
Week 3: 60% refund
Week 4: 40% refund
Week 5: 20% refund
Week 6 and beyond: No refund

5. ACADEMIC PROBATION AND ENROLLMENT RESTRICTIONS

5.1 Academic Probation
A student is placed on academic probation if their cumulative GPA falls below 2.0. Students on probation may not enroll in more than 12 credits per quarter.

5.2 Graduate Students on Probation
A graduate student is placed on probation if their cumulative GPA falls below 3.0. A student on graduate academic probation may not enroll in new courses until a remediation plan is approved by their graduate program coordinator.

5.3 Probation and Financial Aid
Students on academic probation who also fail to meet Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) requirements lose eligibility for federal financial aid. They may appeal through the Financial Aid Satisfactory Academic Progress Appeal process.

6. REPEATED COURSES

Students may repeat a course in which they received a grade of C- or lower. The new grade replaces the old grade in the GPA calculation, but both grades remain visible on the transcript. A course may not be repeated more than twice.
