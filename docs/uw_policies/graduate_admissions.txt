UNIVERSITY OF WASHINGTON
GRADUATE SCHOOL ADMISSIONS REQUIREMENTS
The Graduate School — Updated October 2023

1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

All applicants to UW graduate programs must satisfy the following minimum requirements. Individual departments may impose higher standards.

1.1 Bachelor's Degree
Applicants must hold a baccalaureate degree from an accredited institution. The degree must be conferred before the start of the first quarter of enrollment.

1.2 GPA Requirement
The Graduate School requires a minimum cumulative undergraduate GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale for the last 90 credits of undergraduate study. Applicants below this threshold may be considered for provisional admission at the department's discretion.

1.3 English Proficiency
Applicants whose native language is not English must demonstrate English proficiency by submitting scores from one of the following:
- TOEFL iBT: minimum score of 92 (no section below 22)
- IELTS Academic: minimum score of 7.0 (no section below 6.0)
- Duolingo English Test: minimum score of 120

2. PROGRAM-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) PhD
- GRE: Not required as of 2022
- Three letters of recommendation from academic or professional references
- Statement of Purpose (1,000–1,500 words) describing research interests and fit with UW faculty
- Research experience strongly recommended
- Typical admitted GPA: 3.7–4.0; typical GRE (historical): Quant 165+

2.2 Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) Master's (Non-Thesis)
- Three letters of recommendation
- Statement of Purpose (500–1,000 words)
- Minimum 3.0 GPA; competitive applicants typically have 3.5+

2.3 Statistics MS/PhD
- Three letters of recommendation
- Statement of Purpose
- GRE Quant strongly recommended (160+ competitive)
- Background in linear algebra, probability, and calculus required

2.4 Information Science (iSchool) MSIM
- Two letters of recommendation
- Resume/CV
- Statement of Purpose
- Work experience preferred but not required

3. RESEARCH ASSISTANTSHIPS (RA)

3.1 Eligibility
Research Assistantships are awarded by individual faculty members and departments, not by the Graduate School centrally. To be eligible, a student must be enrolled full-time (10+ credits) and maintain a graduate GPA of 3.0 or above.

3.2 Compensation
RA positions typically include a tuition waiver for the quarter in which the appointment is held and a monthly stipend. For the 2023–2024 academic year, the standard RA stipend for 50% FTE is $2,850 per month.

3.3 Academic Year vs Summer
RA appointments during the academic year (Autumn, Winter, Spring) are covered under the Graduate School's tuition waiver program. Summer RA appointments require separate departmental funding and do not automatically include a tuition waiver.

3.4 RA and Probation
A student on academic probation (GPA below 3.0) is not eligible to hold an RA appointment. Probationary students must raise their GPA to 3.0 within two quarters or face dismissal from the program.

4. TEACHING ASSISTANTSHIPS (TA)

4.1 Eligibility
TAs must be enrolled full-time and maintain a GPA of 3.0. International TAs must pass the UW English Language Proficiency Exam (ELPE) before teaching independently.

4.2 Compensation
Standard TA compensation for a 50% FTE appointment includes a monthly stipend of $2,650 and a full tuition waiver for that quarter.

5. FELLOWSHIPS AND FUNDING

5.1 Graduate School Fellowship
The Graduate School awards top-up fellowships to students nominated by their department. Award amounts range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

5.2 External Fellowships
Students are encouraged to apply for NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (GRFP), NSERC (for Canadian applicants), and other external funding sources. Award acceptance must be reported to the department within 14 days.

6. DEADLINES

Most PhD programs: December 15 (for Autumn admission)
Most Master's programs: January 15 (for Autumn admission)
International applicants are encouraged to apply 4–6 weeks earlier due to visa processing.
